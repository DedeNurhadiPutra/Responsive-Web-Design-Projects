Sultan Baabullah Ternate

The Ruler of 72 Islands.

Here's a time line of Sultan Baabullah life:

  • 1528 - Born in February 10 Ternate, North Moluccas.
  • 1545 â€“ 1546 - Since childhood he always accompanied his father (Sultan Khairun), even to the point of following him in temporary exile to Goa (India).
  • 1560 - Helped his father to run the affairs of government and the sultanate, and co-signed an act of Vassalage (the oldest preserved Indonesian letter with seals).
  • 1563 - Undertook an expedition to the northern parts of Sulawesi in order to bring these lands under his father's realm.
  • 1570 - The tragic death of Sultan Hairun triggered the anger of the Ternatans as well as the various kings of Maluku.
  • 1570 - Lead the siege of Gammalamo Fort with a force of 2.000 Korakoras fleets and approximately 130.000 soldiers.
  • 1570-1571 - Sultan Baabullah sent a large fleet of 6 Korakoras containing 500 soldiers and led by Kapita Kalasinka to expel the Portuguese who were in Ambon.
  • 1571-1575 - Leading the expulsion of the Portuguese in Buton, Selayar and Makassar.
  • 1575 - Expelling the Portuguese from Ternate and making Ternate the center of the clove trade in Maluku with an international network.
  • 1579 - Sultan Babullah received a visit of Francis Drake (the well-known English seafarer and adventurer) and make diplomatic relations with United Kingdom.
  • 1580-1583 - Sultan Baabullah led a large naval expedition (hongi) which visited a number of places to increase its territory and influence to several areas in Moluccas, Sulawesi, Nusa Tenggara, Papua and Southern Philippines.
  • 1580-1583 - Spain and Portugal Join the Spanish-Portuguese Union. Then Sultan Baabullah put up a fight against Governor of Spain in Manila, Philippines, including : Juan de Morones, Don Gomez Perez Dasmarinas and Gonzalo Ronquillo.
  • 1583 - May 25, Sultan Baabullah died at the age of 55 and was buried in Foramadiahi, Ternate.

"What shall we value the Portuguese if we once become sensible of our own strength? What can we fear, or not dare to attempt? The Portuguese value him who robs most, and is guilty of the greatest crimes and enormities ... Ours are our country, and the defense of our parents, our wives, our children and our liberty."

-- Bartholomew Leonardo de Argensola (1708) The Discovery and Conquest of the Molucco and Philippine Islands. London, p. 54. --
